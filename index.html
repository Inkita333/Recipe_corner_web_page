gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of milk powder
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1/4 cup of ghee (clarified butter)

Recipe

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder and ghee.
  2. Gradually add milk and knead the mixture into a soft dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small portions.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the dough balls.
  5. Prepare sugar syrup by dissolving 1 cup of sugar in 1 cup of water.
  6. Add the fried Gulab Jamun to the syrup, soak for a few hours

Bread Rasmalai

Ingredients

  • 4 slices of bread
  • 2 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of condensed milk

Recipe

  1. Remove the crust from the bread slices
  2. cut them into small pieces.
  3. In a pan, heat the milk and condensed milk together
  4. Add the bread pieces to the milk mixture .
  5. cook for 5-7 minutes until it thickens further and the bread softens
  6. Let it cool, then refrigerate. Serve chilled as Bread Rasmalai.

Samosa

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 2 cups of boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup of green peas
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • salt to taste

Recipe

  1. In a bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, oil, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Add water to make a dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. In a pan, heat a little oil, add cumin seeds
  4. then add the mashed potatoes and spices.
  5. Cook until the mixture is well combined and fragrant.
  6. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each into a thin oval shape.
  7. Cut it in half to form two semi-circles.
  8. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone, and seal the edges with water.
  9. Fill it with the potato mixture then seal the open edges.
  10. Heat oil for deep frying, and fry the samosas.

Dahi Puri

Ingredients

  • Puri shells (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup of plain yogurt (curd)
  • 1/2 cup of tamarind chutney
  • 1/2 cup of boiled and mashed potatoes

Recipe

  1. Take the puri shells and gently crack the top to create an opening.
  2. Fill each puri shell with a small amount of mashed potatoes.
  3. Top the potatoes with a spoonful of plain yogurt (curd).
  4. Drizzle tamarind chutney over the yogurt-filled puris.
  5. Garnish with a pinch of chaat masala or black salt, if desired.
